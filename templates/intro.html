Usage

  • On the left you see the next math problem that you are supposed to solve correctly.
  • Use your mouse to operate iCalculator. Click the digits and operators to input the given math problem.
  • AC lets you start the current calculation over again (in case you mistyped).
  • C allows you to correct only the last digit or operator you clicked.
  • = puts your solution into the answer field.
  • Click SUBMIT to see if your solution is correct and proceed to the next math problem.

Recap of Exponentiation

In case you need help remembering what exponentiation (x2, x3, x4 a. s. o.) actually means, have a short reminder:

  • x2 is equal to x times x
  • x3 is equal to x times x times x
  • x4 is equal to x times x times x times x
  • a. s. o.
So for example:
  • 102 = 100, because it is equal to 10 times 10
  • 103 = 1000, because it is equal to 10 times 10 times 10
